Clever Poster

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

1. Overview

Clever Poster is a self-hosted application. All data is stored locally on the machine running the Application. We do not operate any external servers, analytics services, or data collection infrastructure.

2. Data We Store Locally

  • Google OAuth tokens — stored encrypted in the local PostgreSQL database using AES-128 (Fernet symmetric encryption). Used solely to read video files from your connected Google Drive.
  • TikTok OAuth tokens — stored encrypted in the local database. Used solely to publish videos to the TikTok accounts you have connected.
  • Video metadata — filenames, file sizes, AI-generated descriptions, hashtags, and product links are stored locally to support the review and posting workflow. No video file content is stored permanently; videos are streamed directly from Google Drive.
  • Application settings — preferences such as your selected AI model, timezone, and folder mappings are stored locally.

3. Data Shared with Third Parties

  • Google — OAuth authentication requests are sent to Google's servers. The Application requests read-only access to your Drive and does not transmit Drive content to any party other than TikTok during a post.
  • TikTok — when you post a video, the video file and caption (description, hashtags, product links) are uploaded to TikTok via their Content Posting API. This transmission is initiated explicitly by you.
  • Ollama (local AI) — video frames are passed to the Ollama process running on your local machine. No data is sent to any external AI service or third party.

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with any advertisers or analytics providers.

4. Data Retention & Deletion

All data is stored in the local database managed by you. You can delete all stored data at any time by stopping the Application and removing the Docker volume (docker compose down -v). Revoking Google or TikTok OAuth access from within those platforms' account settings will also invalidate stored tokens.

5. Security

OAuth tokens are encrypted at rest using Fernet symmetric encryption. The encryption key is stored in your local .env file and is never transmitted. The Application is only accessible on your local network and is served over HTTPS using a locally-trusted certificate.

6. Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated at any time. The "last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.
